INTRODUCTION



Aside from being a combat sport, Taekwondo is a martial arts discipline that facilitates students in strengthening their physical and mental attributes. With the aim of producing a conducive learning atmosphere, all APU Taekwondo Club members are encouraged to demonstrate mutual respect to one another to assist with this cause.


The Dojang is the official ground for all Taekwondo training and proceedings. It is considered a sacred space where students can acquire and obtain the authentic methods of Taekwondo. Additionally, it serves as a brief haven from the everyday struggle, demands, and duties of student life. When entering the Dojang, students are expected to evolve into Taekwondo disciples and devote themselves to the training.


It is essential to mention here that the below regulations of Taekwondo rules and etiquette are only for the information and reading of club participants; they are not required to be remembered or memorized. However, each APU Taekwondo Club member must adhere to these guidelines and principles when attending training sessions. Failure to comply with these requirements may result in your expulsion from the club.


RULES AND REGULATIONS


  1. Display respect and civility to all club members.

  2. Distractions to training sessions should be kept to a minimum.

  3. Exercise self-restraint during training over strength or speed.

  4. Always ensure sufficient space between each member when sparring and avoid initiating contact unless advised.

  5. Always attempt new manoeuvres under the observation of senior rank members.

  6. Personal hygiene is vital to ensure the safety of all participants, such as clipped fingers and toenails.

  7. Personal jewellery is not permitted unless the instructor has granted consent.

  8. Mobile devices should be turned off and left unattended throughout the training session.

  9. Lost of personal belongings will be the responsibility of the member, not the club.

  10. Any injury sustained during training should be promptly reported to the Taekwondo instructor.

ETIQUETTE


  1. While entering or exiting the Dojang, always greet the flags and instructors.

  2. Senior members should line up in front at the start of a training session, followed by members with mid-level or junior experience.

  3. All Taekwondo instructors should be addressed as either "Sir" or "Madam" during training. Likewise, all training attendees should address Dan-level members by their surnames.

  4. Kindly select a suitable opportunity to avoid disrupting the training session if a concerning question needs to be mentioned.

  5. It is customary to seek permission from the Taekwondo instructor before entering the Dojang if you have arrived late for a training session. In that event, place yourself discreetly to the side, lift your arm, and patiently standby until the instructor notices you.

  6. Maintain a clean and clutter-free Dojang environment at all times.

  7. Always make a conservative effort to improve yourself and assist other members.

  8. Do not abruptly quit training before the conclusion of the training session without first obtaining permission from the Taekwondo instructor.

  9. Kindly maintain the cleanliness and tidiness of your "Dobok". At all times, each member should tuck their jacket into the trouser to ensure neatness during the training session.

  10. Your Taekwondo belt represents your standing among your peers. Kindly keep it clean and untorn, and handle it with tremendous reverence and care.
